Justworks Pricing (2026)

Starting at $59+/employee/mo

Justworks starts at $59+/employee/mo, which puts it mid-range for hr payroll. We score it 7.5/10 in our review. The real question for founders is whether the price matches the value, and that answer depends on which tier you pick and how big your team is.

The quick read on Justworks: PEO-model HR that's simpler than traditional providers. Justworks acts as a co-employer (PEO), which means they handle payroll, benefits, compliance, and HR administration under their umbrella. You get enterprise-level benefits at small-business prices. The tradeoff is less flexibility than standalone tools like Gusto or Rippling.

Justworks Plans Explained

Here's what you actually get at each tier, and which plan fits your stage.

Payroll — $59/employee/mo

At $59 per user per month, Payroll is the real starting line for most paid hr payroll buyers. A 5-person team lands at $295/mo, a 10-person team at $590/mo. You get the core product, but expect feature caps that push you toward the next tier within 6-12 months of serious use.

PEO — $109/employee/mo

PEO runs $109 per user per month, which puts a 10-person team at $1,090/mo before any add-ons. This tier is for teams that genuinely need enterprise controls like SSO, audit logs, and custom workflows. If you're not sure you need them, you don't.

What's Included in Justworks Pricing

Every plan includes the core Justworks feature set. Here's what you get access to on paid tiers:

  • PEO services
  • Payroll
  • Benefits (medical, dental, vision)
  • Compliance
  • Workers comp
  • 401(k)

Feature depth grows with the tier. Entry plans cap on automation, integrations, or usage limits. Upper plans unlock the heavier features that mid-market teams actually need. Read the vendor's feature matrix before picking a tier, especially if one specific feature is the reason you're buying.

What to Watch Out For

The most common pricing complaints buyers raise about Justworks:

  • More expensive than DIY platforms like Gusto
  • PEO model means less control over HR decisions
  • Limited customization for unique HR workflows

None of these are deal-breakers on their own. They're the things you want to negotiate or plan around before you sign a contract. The worst time to discover an add-on fee is month three.

The Sultan's Verdict on Justworks Pricing

Justworks lands in the solid-but-not-exceptional zone. The 7.5/10 score reflects a product that does its job well, but there are cheaper tools that cover the same ground and pricier tools that do more. Whether $59+/employee/mo is worth it depends on how closely your workflow matches what Justworks is built for.

The fit test is simple. Justworks is built for small businesses that want enterprise benefits and outsourced hr compliance. If that's you, the pricing is worth it. If it's not, you'll end up paying for features you never touch while missing features you actually need. Buy the tool that fits your motion, not the one with the best pricing page.

The bottom line: Justworks's pricing is defensible if you actually use what it's good at. Its biggest strength is access to enterprise-tier health insurance and benefits, and that's where the money goes. If that strength maps to a real pain point in your business, pay the price. If not, walk away and pick something cheaper.

Justworks Pricing FAQs

How much does Justworks cost?

Justworks starts at $59+/employee/mo. The paid plans scale up from there based on features, seats, or usage. Check the pricing table above for the full tier breakdown.

Does Justworks have a free trial?

Justworks doesn't lead with a free plan, so check the vendor site for current trial terms. Most tools in this category offer a 14-day trial, and some let you demo the product before signing up.

How much does Justworks cost for a 10-person team?

On the Payroll plan at $59 per user per month, a 10-person team pays $590/mo ($7,080/year). Add more for higher tiers or usage-based features.

Are there hidden costs with Justworks?

The biggest gotcha buyers report: more expensive than diy platforms like gusto. Read the contract line items before signing, and ask for the full cost including onboarding and add-ons.

Can you negotiate Justworks pricing?

Small teams usually can't move list prices much, but annual commits, multi-seat deals, and end-of-quarter timing all give you room to push back. Ask for an annual discount and any waived onboarding fees before you sign.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Justworks?

The cheapest real alternative is Deel at Free (contractors) / $599+/mo (EOR). That's well under Justworks's $59+/employee/mo. Don't switch just for the savings. Compare what you'd give up in our Deel review.
