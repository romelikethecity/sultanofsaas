Xero Pricing (2026)

Starting at $15+/mo

Xero is priced starting at $15+/mo, sitting mid-range in accounting. It scores 7.8/10 in our overall review. This page unpacks what each plan actually gets you, what the real monthly spend looks like at different team sizes, and where Xero's pricing earns its keep or fails to.

The quick read on Xero: Clean, modern accounting that works globally. Xero's multi-currency support, unlimited users on all plans, and polished UI make it the better choice for most small businesses outside the US. Even in the US, Xero's lower starting price and cleaner experience give QuickBooks real competition.

Xero Plans Explained

Each tier in plain English. What unlocks at each level, and when to upgrade.

Starter — $15/mo

Starter at $15/mo is the minimum paid commitment. It unlocks Xero's core feature set and moves you past any free-plan limits. For a small team that knows it will use the product daily, this tier usually earns its keep inside the first month.

Standard — $42/mo

At $42/mo, Standard is the tier Xero wants you to pick. It fills the gaps in the entry plan and adds the integrations and automation that most teams discover they need in week two. Budget for this from day one if you're serious about using Xero.

Premium — $78/mo

At $78/mo, Premium is the ceiling of the self-serve pricing. It bundles in the features teams ask for after they hit scale. Good value if you actually use them, expensive padding if you don't.

What's Included in Xero Pricing

Every plan includes the core Xero feature set. Here's what you get access to on paid tiers:

  • Invoicing
  • Bank reconciliation
  • Multi-currency
  • Project tracking
  • Expense claims
  • Payroll (region-dependent)

Feature depth grows with the tier. Entry plans cap on automation, integrations, or usage limits. Upper plans unlock the heavier features that mid-market teams actually need. Read the vendor's feature matrix before picking a tier, especially if one specific feature is the reason you're buying.

What to Watch Out For

The most common pricing complaints buyers raise about Xero:

  • US tax integration isn't as deep as QuickBooks
  • Fewer US-specific payroll options
  • Some US accountants aren't as familiar with it

None of these are deal-breakers on their own. They're the things you want to negotiate or plan around before you sign a contract. The worst time to discover an add-on fee is month three.

The Sultan's Verdict on Xero Pricing

Xero lands in the solid-but-not-exceptional zone. The 7.8/10 score reflects a product that does its job well, but there are cheaper tools that cover the same ground and pricier tools that do more. Whether $15+/mo is worth it depends on how closely your workflow matches what Xero is built for.

The fit test is simple. Xero is built for small businesses that want modern, affordable accounting with multi-currency support. If that's you, the pricing is worth it. If it's not, you'll end up paying for features you never touch while missing features you actually need. Buy the tool that fits your motion, not the one with the best pricing page.

The bottom line: Xero's pricing is defensible if you actually use what it's good at. Its biggest strength is unlimited users on all plans (quickbooks charges per user), and that's where the money goes. If that strength maps to a real pain point in your business, pay the price. If not, walk away and pick something cheaper.

Xero Pricing FAQs

How much does Xero cost?

Xero starts at $15+/mo. The paid plans scale up from there based on features, seats, or usage. Check the pricing table above for the full tier breakdown.

Does Xero have a free trial?

Xero doesn't lead with a free plan, so check the vendor site for current trial terms. Most tools in this category offer a 14-day trial, and some let you demo the product before signing up.

Does Xero charge per user?

No, Xero's Starter plan is a flat platform fee of $15/mo. You can add team members without the per-seat pricing that most SaaS tools use.

Are there hidden costs with Xero?

Watch for add-on modules, onboarding fees, and minimum contract lengths on annual plans. These are common in this category and often aren't visible on the public pricing page.

Is Xero cheaper if you pay annually?

Yes, like most tools in this space, Xero typically discounts annual plans by 15-20%. If the public page only shows monthly, email sales and ask. Founders on tight runway should take the annual cut; everyone else should still consider it.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Xero?

Wave is the budget alternative worth looking at first. It begins at Free compared to Xero's $15+/mo. Feature parity isn't perfect, so read the Wave review before switching.
