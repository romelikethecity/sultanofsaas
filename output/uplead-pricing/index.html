UpLead Pricing (2026)

Starting at $74/mo

UpLead is priced starting at $74/mo, sitting in the middle of the pack in data enrichment. It scores 6.8/10 in our overall review. This page unpacks what each plan actually gets you, what the real monthly spend looks like at different team sizes, and where UpLead's pricing earns its keep or fails to.

The quick read on UpLead: A cleaner, more affordable alternative to ZoomInfo for basic contact lookup. 155M+ contacts with real-time email verification. The interface is simple, the pricing is transparent, and the data is decent. Won't match ZoomInfo's depth, but delivers 70% of the value at 20% of the price.

UpLead Plans Explained

Each tier in plain English. What unlocks at each level, and when to upgrade.

Essentials — $74/mo

Essentials at $74/mo is the minimum paid commitment. It unlocks UpLead's core feature set and moves you past any free-plan limits. For a small team that knows it will use the product daily, this tier usually earns its keep inside the first month.

Plus — $149/mo

At $149/mo, Plus is the tier UpLead wants you to pick. It fills the gaps in the entry plan and adds the integrations and automation that most teams discover they need in week two. Budget for this from day one if you're serious about using UpLead.

Professional — $299/mo

Professional costs $299/mo. It's a step-up from the base plan, typically with more automation, higher limits, or advanced integrations. Worth it only if you can point to a specific feature on this tier that you actually need.

Enterprise — Custom

The custom-quote tier means you're into sales-led territory. Expect discovery calls, annual contracts, and a price that scales with your seat count and feature needs. If you're here, build a spreadsheet of alternatives before the first call.

What's Included in UpLead Pricing

Every plan includes the core UpLead feature set. Here's what you get access to on paid tiers:

  • Contact search
  • Email verification
  • Company data
  • Technographics
  • Intent data (basic)
  • CRM integrations

Feature depth grows with the tier. Entry plans cap on automation, integrations, or usage limits. Upper plans unlock the heavier features that mid-market teams actually need. Read the vendor's feature matrix before picking a tier, especially if one specific feature is the reason you're buying.

What to Watch Out For

The most common pricing complaints buyers raise about UpLead:

  • Smaller database than ZoomInfo or Apollo
  • Limited intent data and advanced features
  • Phone number coverage is weaker

None of these are deal-breakers on their own. They're the things you want to negotiate or plan around before you sign a contract. The worst time to discover an add-on fee is month three.

The Sultan's Verdict on UpLead Pricing

UpLead scores 6.8/10, which is a reminder that the price tag isn't the whole picture. You're paying $74/mo for a product with real limitations, and the cons matter. Before committing, check the alternatives above. At this score, you need a specific reason to pick UpLead over the leaders in data enrichment.

The fit test is simple. UpLead is built for smbs wanting affordable b2b contact data without zoominfo pricing. If that's you, the pricing is worth it. If it's not, you'll end up paying for features you never touch while missing features you actually need. Buy the tool that fits your motion, not the one with the best pricing page.

The bottom line: UpLead's pricing is defensible if you actually use what it's good at. Its biggest strength is transparent, affordable pricing, and that's where the money goes. If that strength maps to a real pain point in your business, pay the price. If not, walk away and pick something cheaper.

UpLead Pricing FAQs

How much does UpLead cost?

UpLead starts at $74/mo. The paid plans scale up from there based on features, seats, or usage. Check the pricing table above for the full tier breakdown.

Does UpLead have a free trial?

UpLead doesn't lead with a free plan, so check the vendor site for current trial terms. Most tools in this category offer a 14-day trial, and some let you demo the product before signing up.

Does UpLead charge per user?

No, UpLead's Essentials plan is a flat platform fee of $74/mo. You can add team members without the per-seat pricing that most SaaS tools use.

Are there hidden costs with UpLead?

Watch for add-on modules, onboarding fees, and minimum contract lengths on annual plans. These are common in this category and often aren't visible on the public pricing page.

Is UpLead cheaper if you pay annually?

Yes, like most tools in this space, UpLead typically discounts annual plans by 15-20%. If the public page only shows monthly, email sales and ask. Founders on tight runway should take the annual cut; everyone else should still consider it.

Is there a cheaper alternative to UpLead?

Lusha is the budget alternative worth looking at first. It begins at $29/user/mo compared to UpLead's $74/mo. Feature parity isn't perfect, so read the Lusha review before switching.
