SalesLoft Pricing (2026)

Starting at $75/user/mo

SalesLoft starts at $75/user/mo, which puts it on the expensive side for sales engagement. We score it 7.5/10 in our review. The real question for founders is whether the price matches the value, and that answer depends on which tier you pick and how big your team is.

The quick read on SalesLoft: Outreach's main competitor, now merged with Clari. Strong sequencing and coaching features, slightly more intuitive than Outreach. The Clari merger adds forecasting but introduces uncertainty about product direction. A solid platform navigating a complex transition.

SalesLoft Plans Explained

Here's what you actually get at each tier, and which plan fits your stage.

Essentials — $75/user/mo

At $75 per user per month, Essentials is the real starting line for most paid sales engagement buyers. A 5-person team lands at $375/mo, a 10-person team at $750/mo. You get the core product, but expect feature caps that push you toward the next tier within 6-12 months of serious use.

Advanced — $125/user/mo

Advanced is where most growing teams settle. At $125 per user per month, a 10-person team pays $1,250/mo and a 25-person team pays $3,125/mo. You get more automation, better reporting, and the features that make SalesLoft actually worth paying for.

Premier — Custom

The custom-quote tier means you're into sales-led territory. Expect discovery calls, annual contracts, and a price that scales with your seat count and feature needs. If you're here, build a spreadsheet of alternatives before the first call.

What's Included in SalesLoft Pricing

Every plan includes the core SalesLoft feature set. Here's what you get access to on paid tiers:

  • Email sequences
  • Dialer
  • Coaching
  • Pipeline management
  • Forecasting (Clari)
  • Analytics

Feature depth grows with the tier. Entry plans cap on automation, integrations, or usage limits. Upper plans unlock the heavier features that mid-market teams actually need. Read the vendor's feature matrix before picking a tier, especially if one specific feature is the reason you're buying.

What to Watch Out For

The most common pricing complaints buyers raise about SalesLoft:

  • Still expensive for SMBs
  • Post-merger product direction is uncertain
  • Some feature overlap with Clari creates confusion

None of these are deal-breakers on their own. They're the things you want to negotiate or plan around before you sign a contract. The worst time to discover an add-on fee is month three.

The Sultan's Verdict on SalesLoft Pricing

SalesLoft lands in the solid-but-not-exceptional zone. The 7.5/10 score reflects a product that does its job well, but there are cheaper tools that cover the same ground and pricier tools that do more. Whether $75/user/mo is worth it depends on how closely your workflow matches what SalesLoft is built for.

The fit test is simple. SalesLoft is built for sales teams wanting engagement + forecasting in one platform. If that's you, the pricing is worth it. If it's not, you'll end up paying for features you never touch while missing features you actually need. Buy the tool that fits your motion, not the one with the best pricing page.

The bottom line: SalesLoft's pricing is defensible if you actually use what it's good at. Its biggest strength is more intuitive ui than outreach, and that's where the money goes. If that strength maps to a real pain point in your business, pay the price. If not, walk away and pick something cheaper.

SalesLoft Pricing FAQs

How much does SalesLoft cost?

SalesLoft starts at $75/user/mo. The paid plans scale up from there based on features, seats, or usage. Check the pricing table above for the full tier breakdown.

Does SalesLoft have a free trial?

SalesLoft doesn't lead with a free plan, so check the vendor site for current trial terms. Most tools in this category offer a 14-day trial, and some let you demo the product before signing up.

How much does SalesLoft cost for a 10-person team?

On the Essentials plan at $75 per user per month, a 10-person team pays $750/mo ($9,000/year). Add more for higher tiers or usage-based features.

Are there hidden costs with SalesLoft?

The biggest gotcha buyers report: still expensive for smbs. Read the contract line items before signing, and ask for the full cost including onboarding and add-ons.

Is SalesLoft cheaper if you pay annually?

Yes, like most tools in this space, SalesLoft typically discounts annual plans by 15-20%. If the public page only shows monthly, email sales and ask. Founders on tight runway should take the annual cut; everyone else should still consider it.

Is there a cheaper alternative to SalesLoft?

Yesware is the budget alternative worth looking at first. It begins at $15/user/mo compared to SalesLoft's $75/user/mo. Feature parity isn't perfect, so read the Yesware review before switching.
