Best Linear Alternatives (2026)

Linear is built for engineering teams. If your team includes non-engineers or you need features beyond issue tracking, these alternatives are broader.

The most common complaints about Linear include only useful for software teams, limited outside of issue tracking, fewer integrations than asana/monday. These aren't minor gripes. They're the kind of friction that pushes teams to evaluate other options after months of trying to make it work.

Linear scores 8.6/10 in our review, which means it's a solid tool for its target audience. But if you're not in that target audience (Engineering and product teams who care about speed and craft), you'll feel the gaps quickly.

The Bottom Line

If you're leaving Linear, Asana is the strongest overall replacement. It scores 8.4/10 and starts at Free / $10.99/user/mo. ClickUp is a close second, especially if you're looking for teams who want one tool to replace everything. Don't just pick the highest-scored option though. Match the tool to your workflow, your team size, and your budget. The best Linear alternative is the one that solves the specific problem that made you start looking in the first place.

What is the best Linear alternative in 2026?

The Sultan's top pick is Asana (scored 8.4/10). It offers a strong feature set at a competitive price point starting at Free / $10.99/user/mo.

Why do people switch away from Linear?

Common reasons include pricing changes, missing features for specific workflows, and team size constraints. Linear scores 8.6/10 overall, which means there are clear areas where alternatives can outperform it.

Is it hard to migrate away from Linear?

Most Linear alternatives offer import tools or migration guides. Plan for 1-3 weeks depending on your data volume and the number of integrations you need to reconnect.
