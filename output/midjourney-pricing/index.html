Midjourney Pricing (2026)

Starting at $10/mo

Midjourney starts at $10/mo, which puts it on the budget end for ai tools. We score it 7.5/10 in our review. The real question for founders is whether the price matches the value, and that answer depends on which tier you pick and how big your team is.

The quick read on Midjourney: The best AI image generator for quality and aesthetics. Midjourney produces visuals that look professional enough for marketing materials, social posts, and presentations. The Discord interface is clunky, but the output quality makes up for it.

Midjourney Plans Explained

Here's what you actually get at each tier, and which plan fits your stage.

Basic — $10/mo

Basic at $10/mo is the minimum paid commitment. It unlocks Midjourney's core feature set and moves you past any free-plan limits. For a small team that knows it will use the product daily, this tier usually earns its keep inside the first month.

Standard — $30/mo

At $30/mo, Standard is the tier Midjourney wants you to pick. It fills the gaps in the entry plan and adds the integrations and automation that most teams discover they need in week two. Budget for this from day one if you're serious about using Midjourney.

Pro — $60/mo

At $60/mo, Pro is the ceiling of the self-serve pricing. It bundles in the features teams ask for after they hit scale. Good value if you actually use them, expensive padding if you don't.

What's Included in Midjourney Pricing

Every plan includes the core Midjourney feature set. Here's what you get access to on paid tiers:

  • Text-to-image generation
  • Image variations
  • Upscaling
  • Style references
  • Zoom out
  • Pan and extend

Feature depth grows with the tier. Entry plans cap on automation, integrations, or usage limits. Upper plans unlock the heavier features that mid-market teams actually need. Read the vendor's feature matrix before picking a tier, especially if one specific feature is the reason you're buying.

What to Watch Out For

The most common pricing complaints buyers raise about Midjourney:

  • Discord-only interface is awkward for business use
  • Learning prompt engineering takes time
  • No API for automation

None of these are deal-breakers on their own. They're the things you want to negotiate or plan around before you sign a contract. The worst time to discover an add-on fee is month three.

The Sultan's Verdict on Midjourney Pricing

Midjourney lands in the solid-but-not-exceptional zone. The 7.5/10 score reflects a product that does its job well, but there are cheaper tools that cover the same ground and pricier tools that do more. Whether $10/mo is worth it depends on how closely your workflow matches what Midjourney is built for.

The fit test is simple. Midjourney is built for small businesses that need professional-looking visuals without hiring a designer. If that's you, the pricing is worth it. If it's not, you'll end up paying for features you never touch while missing features you actually need. Buy the tool that fits your motion, not the one with the best pricing page.

The bottom line: Midjourney's pricing is defensible if you actually use what it's good at. Its biggest strength is sharpest image quality of any ai generator we tested, and that's where the money goes. If that strength maps to a real pain point in your business, pay the price. If not, walk away and pick something cheaper.

Midjourney Pricing FAQs

How much does Midjourney cost?

Midjourney starts at $10/mo. The paid plans scale up from there based on features, seats, or usage. Check the pricing table above for the full tier breakdown.

Does Midjourney have a free trial?

Midjourney doesn't lead with a free plan, so check the vendor site for current trial terms. Most tools in this category offer a 14-day trial, and some let you demo the product before signing up.

Does Midjourney charge per user?

No, Midjourney's Basic plan is a flat platform fee of $10/mo. You can add team members without the per-seat pricing that most SaaS tools use.

Are there hidden costs with Midjourney?

Watch for add-on modules, onboarding fees, and minimum contract lengths on annual plans. These are common in this category and often aren't visible on the public pricing page.

Is Midjourney cheaper if you pay annually?

Yes, like most tools in this space, Midjourney typically discounts annual plans by 15-20%. If the public page only shows monthly, email sales and ask. Founders on tight runway should take the annual cut; everyone else should still consider it.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Midjourney?

ChatGPT / Claude is the budget alternative worth looking at first. It begins at Free / $20/mo compared to Midjourney's $10/mo. Feature parity isn't perfect, so read the ChatGPT / Claude review before switching.
