Outplay Plans Explained

A tier-by-tier walkthrough of the Outplay pricing ladder.

Growth — $79/mo/user

At $79 per user per month, Growth is the real starting line for most paid ai sdr buyers. A 5-person team lands at $395/mo, a 10-person team at $790/mo. You get the core product, but expect feature caps that push you toward the next tier within 6-12 months of serious use.

Enterprise — $120/mo/user

Enterprise is where most growing teams settle. At $120 per user per month, a 10-person team pays $1,200/mo and a 25-person team pays $3,000/mo. You get more automation, better reporting, and the features that make Outplay actually worth paying for.

Custom — Custom

The custom-quote tier means you're into sales-led territory. Expect discovery calls, annual contracts, and a price that scales with your seat count and feature needs. If you're here, build a spreadsheet of alternatives before the first call.

Feature depth grows with the tier. Entry plans cap on automation, integrations, or usage limits. Upper plans unlock the heavier features that mid-market teams actually need. Read the vendor's feature matrix before picking a tier, especially if one specific feature is the reason you're buying.

What to Watch Out For

The most common pricing complaints buyers raise about Outplay:

  • AI features are additions, not core
  • Less polished than enterprise competitors
  • Smaller ecosystem and fewer integrations

None of these are deal-breakers on their own. They're the things you want to negotiate or plan around before you sign a contract. The worst time to discover an add-on fee is month three.

The Sultan's Verdict on Outplay Pricing

Outplay scores 6.8/10, which is a reminder that the price tag isn't the whole picture. You're paying $79/mo for a product with real limitations, and the cons matter. Before committing, check the alternatives above. At this score, you need a specific reason to pick Outplay over the leaders in ai sdr.

The fit test is simple. Outplay is built for smb sales teams wanting multi-channel outreach without enterprise pricing. If that's you, the pricing is worth it. If it's not, you'll end up paying for features you never touch while missing features you actually need. Buy the tool that fits your motion, not the one with the best pricing page.

The bottom line: Outplay's pricing is defensible if you actually use what it's good at. Its biggest strength is true multi-channel: email, phone, linkedin, sms, and that's where the money goes. If that strength maps to a real pain point in your business, pay the price. If not, walk away and pick something cheaper.

Outplay Pricing FAQs

How much does Outplay cost?

Outplay starts at $79/mo. The paid plans scale up from there based on features, seats, or usage. Check the pricing table above for the full tier breakdown.

Does Outplay have a free trial?

Outplay doesn't lead with a free plan, so check the vendor site for current trial terms. Most tools in this category offer a 14-day trial, and some let you demo the product before signing up.

How much does Outplay cost for a 10-person team?

On the Growth plan at $79 per user per month, a 10-person team pays $790/mo ($9,480/year). Add more for higher tiers or usage-based features.

Are there hidden costs with Outplay?

Watch for add-on modules, onboarding fees, and minimum contract lengths on annual plans. These are common in this category and often aren't visible on the public pricing page.

Is Outplay cheaper if you pay annually?

Yes, like most tools in this space, Outplay typically discounts annual plans by 15-20%. If the public page only shows monthly, email sales and ask. Founders on tight runway should take the annual cut; everyone else should still consider it.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Outplay?

Lavender is the budget alternative worth looking at first. It begins at $29/mo compared to Outplay's $79/mo. Feature parity isn't perfect, so read the Lavender review before switching.
