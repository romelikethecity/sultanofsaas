Paychex Pricing (2026)

Starting at $39+/mo + $5/employee

Pricing for Paychex begins at $39+/mo + $5/employee. That's in the middle of the pack compared to the rest of the hr payroll space. Our review gives it a 7.0/10. Below is the tier-by-tier breakdown, the math for different team sizes, and the parts of the pricing model that deserve closer scrutiny before you commit.

The quick read on Paychex: Traditional payroll provider that's been around since 1971. Paychex serves 740,000+ businesses and offers payroll, HR, benefits, and retirement services. The product works but the experience feels dated compared to Gusto or Rippling. Best for businesses that want an established provider with local sales reps and phone support.

Paychex Plans Explained

Breaking down every Paychex plan, what's included, and who should pay for it.

Essentials — $39/mo + $5/employee

At $39 per user per month, Essentials is the real starting line for most paid hr payroll buyers. A 5-person team lands at $195/mo, a 10-person team at $390/mo. You get the core product, but expect feature caps that push you toward the next tier within 6-12 months of serious use.

Select — Custom

What's Included in Paychex Pricing

Every plan includes the core Paychex feature set. Here's what you get access to on paid tiers:

  • Payroll
  • Tax filing
  • HR administration
  • Benefits
  • Retirement (401k)
  • Workers comp

Feature depth grows with the tier. Entry plans cap on automation, integrations, or usage limits. Upper plans unlock the heavier features that mid-market teams actually need. Read the vendor's feature matrix before picking a tier, especially if one specific feature is the reason you're buying.

What to Watch Out For

The most common pricing complaints buyers raise about Paychex:

  • Interface feels dated compared to modern competitors
  • Pricing isn't transparent (requires a quote for most plans)
  • Add-on fees can surprise you

None of these are deal-breakers on their own. They're the things you want to negotiate or plan around before you sign a contract. The worst time to discover an add-on fee is month three.

The Sultan's Verdict on Paychex Pricing

Paychex scores 7.0/10, which is a reminder that the price tag isn't the whole picture. You're paying $39+/mo + $5/employee for a product with real limitations, and the cons matter. Before committing, check the alternatives above. At this score, you need a specific reason to pick Paychex over the leaders in hr payroll.

The fit test is simple. Paychex is built for traditional businesses that want an established payroll provider with hands-on support. If that's you, the pricing is worth it. If it's not, you'll end up paying for features you never touch while missing features you actually need. Buy the tool that fits your motion, not the one with the best pricing page.

The bottom line: Paychex's pricing is defensible if you actually use what it's good at. Its biggest strength is established provider with 50+ years of history, and that's where the money goes. If that strength maps to a real pain point in your business, pay the price. If not, walk away and pick something cheaper.

Paychex Pricing FAQs

How much does Paychex cost?

Paychex starts at $39+/mo + $5/employee. The paid plans scale up from there based on features, seats, or usage. Check the pricing table above for the full tier breakdown.

Does Paychex have a free trial?

Paychex doesn't lead with a free plan, so check the vendor site for current trial terms. Most tools in this category offer a 14-day trial, and some let you demo the product before signing up.

How much does Paychex cost for a 10-person team?

On the Essentials plan at $39 per user per month, a 10-person team pays $390/mo ($4,680/year). Add more for higher tiers or usage-based features.

Are there hidden costs with Paychex?

The biggest gotcha buyers report: interface feels dated compared to modern competitors. Read the contract line items before signing, and ask for the full cost including onboarding and add-ons.

Is Paychex cheaper if you pay annually?

Yes, like most tools in this space, Paychex typically discounts annual plans by 15-20%. If the public page only shows monthly, email sales and ask. Founders on tight runway should take the annual cut; everyone else should still consider it.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Paychex?

Deel is the budget alternative worth looking at first. It begins at Free (contractors) / $599+/mo (EOR) compared to Paychex's $39+/mo + $5/employee. Feature parity isn't perfect, so read the Deel review before switching.
