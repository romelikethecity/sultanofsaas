Drip Pricing (2026)

Starting at $39/mo

Drip starts at $39/mo, which puts it in the middle of the pack for email marketing. We score it 7.8/10 in our review. The real question for founders is whether the price matches the value, and that answer depends on which tier you pick and how big your team is.

The quick read on Drip: E-commerce email marketing done right. Deep Shopify and WooCommerce integrations, pre-built revenue-focused automations, and segmentation based on purchase behavior.

Drip Plans Explained

Here's what you actually get at each tier, and which plan fits your stage.

Standard — $39/mo (2,500 contacts)

Standard at $39/mo is the minimum paid commitment. It unlocks Drip's core feature set and moves you past any free-plan limits. For a small team that knows it will use the product daily, this tier usually earns its keep inside the first month.

What's Included in Drip Pricing

Every plan includes the core Drip feature set. Here's what you get access to on paid tiers:

  • E-commerce automations
  • Revenue attribution
  • Shopify integration
  • Behavioral segmentation
  • A/B testing
  • Dynamic content

Feature depth grows with the tier. Entry plans cap on automation, integrations, or usage limits. Upper plans unlock the heavier features that mid-market teams actually need. Read the vendor's feature matrix before picking a tier, especially if one specific feature is the reason you're buying.

What to Watch Out For

The most common pricing complaints buyers raise about Drip:

  • More expensive than generalist tools
  • Only useful for e-commerce
  • Limited outside of its niche

None of these are deal-breakers on their own. They're the things you want to negotiate or plan around before you sign a contract. The worst time to discover an add-on fee is month three.

The Sultan's Verdict on Drip Pricing

Drip lands in the solid-but-not-exceptional zone. The 7.8/10 score reflects a product that does its job well, but there are cheaper tools that cover the same ground and pricier tools that do more. Whether $39/mo is worth it depends on how closely your workflow matches what Drip is built for.

The fit test is simple. Drip is built for e-commerce stores on shopify or woocommerce. If that's you, the pricing is worth it. If it's not, you'll end up paying for features you never touch while missing features you actually need. Buy the tool that fits your motion, not the one with the best pricing page.

The bottom line: Drip's pricing is defensible if you actually use what it's good at. Its biggest strength is deep e-commerce integrations, and that's where the money goes. If that strength maps to a real pain point in your business, pay the price. If not, walk away and pick something cheaper.

Drip Pricing FAQs

How much does Drip cost?

Drip starts at $39/mo. The paid plans scale up from there based on features, seats, or usage. Check the pricing table above for the full tier breakdown.

Does Drip have a free trial?

Drip doesn't lead with a free plan, so check the vendor site for current trial terms. Most tools in this category offer a 14-day trial, and some let you demo the product before signing up.

Does Drip charge per user?

No, Drip's Standard plan is a flat platform fee of $39/mo (2,500 contacts). You can add team members without the per-seat pricing that most SaaS tools use.

Are there hidden costs with Drip?

The biggest gotcha buyers report: more expensive than generalist tools. Read the contract line items before signing, and ask for the full cost including onboarding and add-ons.

Can you negotiate Drip pricing?

Small teams usually can't move list prices much, but annual commits, multi-seat deals, and end-of-quarter timing all give you room to push back. Ask for an annual discount and any waived onboarding fees before you sign.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Drip?

The cheapest real alternative is Mailchimp at Free / $13/mo. That's well under Drip's $39/mo. Don't switch just for the savings. Compare what you'd give up in our Mailchimp review.
