Klaviyo Pricing (2026)

Starting at Free / $20/mo

The entry price for Klaviyo is Free / $20/mo, placing it on the expensive side among email marketing tools. Klaviyo Plans Explained

A tier-by-tier walkthrough of the Klaviyo pricing ladder.

Free — $0 (250 contacts)

The free plan is the honest starting point. You can set up Klaviyo, connect it to your workflow, and get real use out of it without handing over a credit card. For solo founders and tiny teams, this is often all you need for the first 6-12 months.

Email — $20/mo

At $20/mo, Email is the tier Klaviyo wants you to pick. It fills the gaps in the entry plan and adds the integrations and automation that most teams discover they need in week two. Budget for this from day one if you're serious about using Klaviyo.

Email + SMS — $35/mo

At $35/mo, Email + SMS is the ceiling of the self-serve pricing. It bundles in the features teams ask for after they hit scale. Good value if you actually use them, expensive padding if you don't.

What's Included in Klaviyo Pricing

Every plan includes the core Klaviyo feature set. Here's what you get access to on paid tiers:

  • Email campaigns
  • SMS
  • Shopify integration
  • Predictive analytics
  • Segmentation
  • Product recommendations

Feature depth grows with the tier. Entry plans cap on automation, integrations, or usage limits. Upper plans unlock the heavier features that mid-market teams actually need. Read the vendor's feature matrix before picking a tier, especially if one specific feature is the reason you're buying.

What to Watch Out For

The most common pricing complaints buyers raise about Klaviyo:

  • Expensive as your list grows
  • Steep learning curve for advanced features
  • Primarily e-commerce focused

None of these are deal-breakers on their own. They're the things you want to negotiate or plan around before you sign a contract. The worst time to discover an add-on fee is month three.

The Sultan's Verdict on Klaviyo Pricing

Klaviyo lands in the solid-but-not-exceptional zone. The 8.2/10 score reflects a product that does its job well, but there are cheaper tools that cover the same ground and pricier tools that do more. Whether Free / $20/mo is worth it depends on how closely your workflow matches what Klaviyo is built for.

The fit test is simple. Klaviyo is built for shopify stores doing $1m+ in revenue who need advanced segmentation. If that's you, the pricing is worth it. If it's not, you'll end up paying for features you never touch while missing features you actually need. Buy the tool that fits your motion, not the one with the best pricing page.

The bottom line: Klaviyo's pricing is defensible if you actually use what it's good at. Its biggest strength is best shopify email integration, and that's where the money goes. If that strength maps to a real pain point in your business, pay the price. If not, walk away and pick something cheaper.

Klaviyo Pricing FAQs

How much does Klaviyo cost?

Klaviyo has a free plan, and the first paid tier is Email at $20/mo. Most teams that outgrow the free tier end up on Email or higher once they hit the free-plan limits.

Is there a free version of Klaviyo?

Yes. Klaviyo offers a free plan that covers the basics. It's a real product, not a time-limited trial, so you can run on it indefinitely if your needs stay small.

Does Klaviyo charge per user?

No, Klaviyo's Email plan is a flat platform fee of $20/mo. You can add team members without the per-seat pricing that most SaaS tools use.

Are there hidden costs with Klaviyo?

The biggest gotcha buyers report: expensive as your list grows. Read the contract line items before signing, and ask for the full cost including onboarding and add-ons.

Does Klaviyo offer annual discounts?

Expect a 15-20% cut for annual prepay, which is standard in this category. Ask sales directly and push for more if you're committing to 10+ seats or multiple years. Monthly billing is the wrong move if you already know you'll stick around.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Klaviyo?

Yes. Mailchimp starts at Free / $13/mo, which undercuts Klaviyo's Free / $20/mo. It's a lighter product in some areas, so audit the feature gap before switching. Our Mailchimp review covers the trade-offs.
